FCFS

Non-preemptive · Arrival order

Definition

FCFS executes processes strictly in the order they arrive — each runs to completion before the next begins. Simple and predictable, but not always efficient.

Advantages

  • Simple to implement
  • No starvation — every process eventually runs
  • Low scheduling overhead

Disadvantages

  • Convoy effect — short jobs wait behind long ones
  • Poor average waiting time
  • Not suitable for interactive systems

ROUND ROBIN

Preemptive · Time-quantum based

Definition

RR assigns each process a fixed time quantum. If a process doesn't finish in time, it's preempted and moved to the back of the ready queue — ensuring fair CPU sharing.

Advantages

  • Fair time-sharing among processes
  • Good response time for short jobs
  • No starvation

Disadvantages

  • High context-switch overhead
  • Performance heavily depends on quantum size
  • Poor throughput for large quantum values
